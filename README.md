# Drafts-and-Analysis
Personal notes, drafts and essays to keep the habit of independent thinking.
## ⏱️ TL;DR
An archive of personal drafts, reading notes, unpolished observations, and personal musings in the Western Canon, macro policy, economics, and law.

---

## 📂 Architecture & Scope
This repository is organized by how the information is processed, rather than by traditional academic subjects.

* **/01_Textual_Analysis:** Analysis of the Western Canon, judicial precedents and academic papers.
* **/02_Macro_and_Policy_Analysis:** Analysis of government policy, social observations, economic phenomena, and law. 
* **/03_Methodologies_and_Frameworks:** Stress tests on the applicability, suitability, and internal logical consistency of methodological and theoretical frameworks.
* **/04_Fragmented_Musings:** Unstructured personal thoughts.

---

## 🧭 How to Navigate
I put tags at the beginning of my notes. If you are looking for something specific, just search for keywords or check the tags at the top of each text.

## 📜 Why This Exists

I’ve recently read many empirical studies, investigation reports and professor feedback; the conclusion points out a quite chilling reality: intellectual atrophy in skills such as logical reasoning, complex problem-solving, and critical thinking is causally associated with pervasive reliance on LLM products.

A question pops into my mind: is such intelligence outsourcing an inevitable feature of the era, implying those mentioned skills are outdated and no longer employable, or, merely a negative externality that society and firms bear?

Regardless of the answer, I insist on reading original texts, writing, debate and discussion not for being employable but just for defending my cognitive sovereignty. 

* **Writing** is the process of thinking, detecting hidden gaps in seemingly reasonable arguments in mind.
* **Reading original texts** is chaos navigation, smelling logic chains in nonlinear thoughts of authors.
* **Debate and discussion** is overloading information processing, recognizing what is valuable and what is noise in limited time.

I choose GitHub for its “compare version” and timestamp function. The striking red-and-green contrast and mental pressure brought by setting it public whether readers will be few or none make this platform a great tool for me to keep thinking against LLM inertia.
